Aviation

Orlando International Airport Terminal C

Value: $2.8 billion Size: 1.8M sq ft Opened: September 2022

Pre-EBIC Experience (2018-2020 at SimRobotics): Jordan held multiple leadership positions including Director of Operations, Chief Modeler, QA/QC Admin, IT Admin, Hardware/Software Consultant, and BIM Manager. In BIM Manager capacity, led team of 4 BIM specialists coordinating 45 trade models across three buildings for Turner-Kiewit JV (Landside) and Hensel Phelps (Airside). Systems included concrete foundations, metal cladding, atrium skylight, tarmac fuel system, runway drainage/grading, Emergency Fuel Shut Off (EFSO), vertical concrete, elevated slabs, boarding pier foundations, masonry, bird netting, miscellaneous metals, gyp wall framing, and acoustical ceiling tiles.

Key Features

  • 1,000-foot skylit Boulevard with 100% automated TSA screening
  • 100% facial recognition gates for enhanced security
  • LEED v4 certification for sustainable design
  • First fully integrated multimodal terminal with Brightline rail

Entertainment

Universal Studios Epic Universe

Opening: May 22, 2025 Size: 750+ acres Duration: 2022-2025

Three-year engagement on Universal's most ambitious theme park project. EBIC worked as subcontractor to Southeast Mechanical and Phillips & Jordan Civil Contractors, providing specialized MEP and civil BIM coordination across three themed lands managed by separate general contractors: Hensel Phelps (How to Train Your Dragon land), Yates Construction (Celestial Park), and DPR Construction (Universal Helios Grand Hotel).

EBIC Scope by Area

  • How to Train Your Dragon: Chilled water piping (GC: Hensel Phelps, Sub: Southeast Mechanical)
  • Universal Helios Hotel: 10-story hotel chilled water piping (GC: DPR, Sub: Southeast Mechanical)
  • Celestial Park: Civil underground utilities - sanitary, storm, potable, FDC (GC: Yates, Sub: Phillips & Jordan)
  • Dragon Land Civil: Underground utilities (GC: Hensel Phelps, Sub: Phillips & Jordan)

Multi-billion dollar investment with EBIC providing BIM coordination for multiple subcontractors across three general contractor territories.

Aviation

Tampa International Airport - Bombardier Office Fitout

Type: Scan-to-BIM As-Built Location: APM Maintenance Bay Contractor: Gomez Construction

Specialized Scan-to-BIM as-built documentation project for Bombardier office fitout located in APM maintenance bay within parking garage structure. Post-retrofit reality capture with full LOD 500 modeling of all trades involved. Project executed by Gomez Construction under Hensel Phelps oversight. EBIC provided comprehensive Scan-to-BIM conversion, managing the complete documentation of this adaptive reuse transformation.

Project Characteristics

  • Post-retrofit reality capture
  • LOD 500 modeling all trades
  • Scan-to-BIM as-built documentation
  • Adaptive reuse within parking garage
